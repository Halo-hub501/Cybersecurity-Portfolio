## Key Terms I Now Understand
- **Threat Actor** — a person or group responsible for carrying out an attack
- **Vulnerability** — a weakness in a system that can be exploited
- **Risk** — the potential for damage when a threat meets a vulnerability
- **CIA Triad** — the three core principles of all security decisions
- **SIEM** — a tool analysts use to monitor and analyze security events
- **Social Engineering** — manipulating people into revealing confidential information
- **Phishing** — a technique used to trick users into providing sensitive data
- **Malware** — malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access

---

## How This Applies to Real Analyst Work

Module 1 taught me that a security analyst's core job is
to protect an organization's data and systems from threats.

Understanding the CIA Triad helps me prioritize what needs
to be protected — whether that means keeping data
confidential, making sure it has not been tampered with,
or ensuring systems stay available for legitimate users.

Knowing the history of cyber attacks also helps me
understand why certain security controls exist today.
Every firewall, every password policy, and every audit
log exists because of a real attack that happened in
the past.

As an analyst I will use this foundational knowledge
every single day — whether investigating an alert,
writing an incident report, or explaining a risk to
a manager who is not technical.

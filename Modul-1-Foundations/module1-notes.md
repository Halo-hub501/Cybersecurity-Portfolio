# Module 1 — Foundations of Cybersecurity
**Date Completed:** March 2026
**Platform:** Google Cybersecurity Certificate (Coursera)

## What I Learned
- What cybersecurity is and why organizations need it
- The CIA Triad — Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
- Common threats — malware, phishing, social engineering
- History of how cyber attacks evolved
- Core responsibilities of a security analyst

## Key Terms I Now Understand
- **Threat Actor** — person or group behind an attack
- **Vulnerability** — weakness that can be exploited
- **Risk** — potential damage when threat meets vulnerability
- **CIA Triad** — the 3 core pillars of all security decisions
- **SIEM** — tool analysts use to monitor security events

## How This Applies to Real Analyst Work
Module 1 taught me that a security analyst's core job is to 
protect an organization's data and systems from threats. 
Understanding the CIA Triad helps me prioritize what needs 
to be protected — whether that's keeping data confidential, 
making sure it hasn't been tampered with, or ensuring systems 
stay available for users.

Knowing the history of cyber attacks also helps me understand 
why certain security controls exist today. Every firewall, 
every password policy, every audit log exists because of a 
real attack that happened in the past.

As an analyst I will use this foundational knowledge every 
single day — whether I'm investigating an alert, writing an 
incident report, or explaining a risk to a manager who isn't 
technical.

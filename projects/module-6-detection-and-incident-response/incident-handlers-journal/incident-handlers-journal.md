| **Description** | Documentation of a ransomware attack targeting a small U.S. healthcare clinic. The incident resulted in full encryption of critical files and a complete shutdown of business operations. |
| **Tool(s) Used** | None — this entry is based on scenario analysis and manual documentation. |

### The 5 W's

**Who caused the incident?**  
An organized group of unethical hackers caused the incident. This group is known to specifically target organizations in the healthcare and transportation industries.

**What happened?**  
Employees received targeted phishing emails containing malicious attachments. Once an employee downloaded the attachment, malware was installed on their computer. The attackers then deployed ransomware that encrypted all critical files across the organization. A ransom note was displayed demanding a large sum of money in exchange for the decryption key. Business operations were completely shut down as employees were unable to access medical records or any software needed to do their jobs.

**When did the incident occur?**  
The incident occurred on a Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m.

**Where did the incident happen?**  
The incident occurred at a small U.S. health care clinic specializing in delivering primary-care services.

**Why did the incident happen?**  
The incident happened because employees downloaded malicious email attachments from phishing emails, allowing the attackers to gain initial access to the network. The lack of employee awareness around phishing threats and insufficient email security controls created the vulnerability that the attackers exploited. Once inside the network, the attackers were able to move freely and deploy ransomware across the organization's systems.

### Additional Notes

- This incident highlights the critical importance of **security awareness training** for all employees, particularly around identifying phishing emails.
- The organization should implement **email filtering** and **attachment sandboxing** to prevent malicious files from reaching employees.
- Healthcare organizations are high-value targets because of the sensitivity of patient data and the urgency to restore operations — making them more likely to pay ransoms.
- Key questions to investigate: Did the organization have backups? Was there an incident response plan in place? How many employees downloaded the malicious attachment?
- This incident likely triggers **HIPAA breach notification** requirements since patient data was compromised.

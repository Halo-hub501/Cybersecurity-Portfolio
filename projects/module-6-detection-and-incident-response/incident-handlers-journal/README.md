### The 5 W's

**Who caused the incident?**
An organized group of unethical hackers known to specifically target organizations in the healthcare and transportation industries.

**What happened?**
Employees received targeted phishing emails containing malicious attachments. Once downloaded, malware was installed on their computers. The attackers then deployed ransomware that encrypted all critical files across the organization. A ransom note was displayed demanding a large sum of money in exchange for the decryption key. Employees were unable to access medical records or any software needed to do their jobs, forcing a complete shutdown of business operations.

**When did the incident occur?**
Tuesday morning at approximately 9:00 a.m.

**Where did the incident happen?**
A small U.S. health care clinic specializing in delivering primary-care services.

**Why did the incident happen?**
Employees downloaded malicious email attachments from targeted phishing emails, giving attackers initial access to the network. Insufficient email security controls and lack of employee security awareness training allowed the phishing emails to reach and be acted upon by employees. Once inside the network, the attackers deployed ransomware freely across the organization's systems.

### Additional Notes

- This incident highlights the critical importance of **security awareness training** for all employees around identifying phishing emails
- The organization should implement **email filtering** and **attachment sandboxing** to prevent malicious files from reaching inboxes
- Healthcare organizations are high-value targets because of sensitive patient data and operational urgency — making them more likely to pay ransoms
- This incident likely triggers **HIPAA breach notification** requirements since patient data was compromised
- Key questions to investigate: Were backups in place? Was there an incident response plan? How many employees downloaded the attachment?
